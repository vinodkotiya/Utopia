Amethyst the Magical Stone

By Wendy Chivers · August 4, 2023

What Is Amethyst?

Amethyst is a purple variety of quartz, ranging in colour from pale lavender to deep violet. It forms inside volcanic rock cavities and can be found on nearly every continent—from the mines of Brazil and Uruguay to deposits in Zambia, South Korea, and right here in Canada. Its name comes from the ancient Greek amethystos, meaning "not intoxicated," reflecting the old belief that the stone could protect its wearer from excess and clouded judgment.

For thousands of years, amethyst has been treasured by cultures around the world. Egyptian royalty wore it as a symbol of power. Tibetan monks carved prayer beads from it. Medieval European soldiers carried it into battle for protection and clarity. Today, it remains one of the most beloved and widely used crystals in spiritual practice.

Metaphysical Properties

Amethyst is best known for its deeply calming energy. It quiets an overactive mind, eases anxiety, and creates space for stillness—making it a natural companion for anyone who struggles to slow down. It resonates strongly with the third eye and crown chakras, supporting intuition, spiritual awareness, and connection to higher consciousness.

Many practitioners also value amethyst for its protective qualities. It's thought to transmute negative energy into love, shield against psychic attack, and help break unhealthy patterns or addictions. Whether you're navigating a stressful period or deepening your meditation practice, amethyst offers steady, grounding support.

How to Use Amethyst

  • Meditation: Hold a piece in your palm or place it on your forehead (third eye) during meditation to deepen your practice and strengthen intuitive insight.
  • Sleep: Place amethyst on your nightstand or under your pillow to promote restful sleep and vivid, meaningful dreams.
  • Jewellery: Wearing amethyst as a pendant, ring, or bracelet keeps its calming energy close to your body throughout the day.
  • Home placement: A cluster or geode in your living space purifies the energy of the room and creates a peaceful atmosphere. Entryways and meditation corners are ideal spots.

Caring for Your Amethyst

Amethyst is relatively hardy (7 on the Mohs scale), but its colour can fade with prolonged exposure to direct sunlight. Store it away from bright windows and cleanse it regularly using moonlight, sage smoke, sound, or running water. To recharge its energy, place it on a selenite plate or set it outside under a full moon overnight.
